Essential Metals

Human bodies use metals in many ways, from the iron that makes our blood red to the calcium in our bones and teeth.
These metals are called “essential metals” because they are essential to our bodies’ functions.
We get these essential metals from the food we eat and the water we drink.
Even essential metals can be toxic at high concentrations.
Click on the essential metals, circled in green, to learn more about their role in the human body.

Toxic Metals

While the essential metals are busy doing good for our bodies (when consumed in the right amounts), other metals can be harmful to health, even in small amounts.
These are called toxic metals.
